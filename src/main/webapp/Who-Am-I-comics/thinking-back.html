New York sped by on fast forward,
dark rooftop water towers
and a dead forest of antennas and chimenys, all a blur.
It was shakedown time
to find out who H. K. really is.
Born in 1989, a small town,
a nice place.
Loving parents, lots of friends--
.. a good kid.
Now he's some computer guy.
The best in DPYN-AED collaborative team--
--good-hearted macho bullshit like that.
Still, missing.
There was no glory in this.
He hadn't asked for this crap. Trouble had come to him, in big dark swarms.
The good and the just were like gold dust in the city. I have no illusions. He was not one of them--
He was no hero.
There is something disturbingly familiar in his records. Written in all pretty curves:
He's in a graphic novel.
The truth split my skull open, a glaring green light washing the lines away. All of his past was just fragmented still shots, words hanging in the air like balloons. He was in a graphic novel. Funny as hell, it is the most horrible thing i could think of.
In a nightmare, every choice you make is the wrong one.
